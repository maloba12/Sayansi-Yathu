Experiment 1: Identification of Laboratory Apparatus
Topic: Introduction to Chemistry
Grade: Form 1

Different apparatus have different shapes and uses.

Laboratory apparatus are designed for specific purposes.

Handle glassware carefully.

TOPIC 2: STATES OF MATTER
==================================================

Experiment 3: Change of State (Melting and Boiling)
Topic: States of Matter
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To observe changes of state.
Materials: Ice, water, beaker, heat source.
Procedure:
1. Heat ice gently until it melts.
2. Continue heating until it boils.
Observation:
Ice melts to water; water boils to steam.
Conclusion:
Heat causes changes in states of matter.
Safety Precautions:
Avoid touching hot equipment.

--------------------------------------------------

Experiment 4: Diffusion in Liquids
Topic: States of Matter
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To demonstrate diffusion in liquids.
Materials: Water, potassium permanganate crystals.
Procedure:
1. Place water in a beaker.
2. Add a crystal of potassium permanganate.
Observation:
Colour spreads through water.
Conclusion:
Particles move from high to low concentration.
Safety Precautions:
Do not touch chemicals directly.

Experiment 7: Air Supports Combustion
Topic: Air and Combustion
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To show that air supports burning.
Materials: Candle, matchstick, glass jar.
Procedure:
1. Light the candle.
2. Cover it with a glass jar.
Observation:
Flame goes out.
Conclusion:
Oxygen in air supports combustion.
Safety Precautions:
Handle fire carefully.

Experiment 8: Properties of Carbon Dioxide
Topic: Air and Combustion
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To test for carbon dioxide.
Materials: Limewater, carbon dioxide gas.
Procedure:
1. Bubble gas through limewater.
Observation:
Limewater turns milky.
Conclusion:
Carbon dioxide reacts with limewater.
Safety Precautions:
Avoid inhaling gases.

TOPIC 6: ACIDS, BASES AND INDICATORS
==================================================

Experiment 11: Litmus Test
Topic: Acids and Bases
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To identify acids and bases.
Materials: Litmus paper, lemon juice, soap solution.
Procedure:
1. Dip litmus paper into substances.
Observation:
Acids turn blue litmus red; bases turn red litmus blue.
Conclusion:
Litmus identifies acids and bases.
Safety Precautions:
Avoid contact with eyes.

--------------------------------------------------

Experiment 12: Natural Indicators
Topic: Acids and Bases
Grade: Form 1
Aim: To prepare a natural indicator.
Materials: Red cabbage leaves, hot water.
Procedure:
1. Soak leaves in hot water.
2. Use solution to test substances.
Observation:
Colour changes occur.
Conclusion:
Plants can act as indicators.
Safety Precautions:
Handle hot water carefully.
